# 📖 Study Strategy — Full Breakdown

## Philosophy

This tracker is built on one principle: **high yield over high volume.**

With 3 hours a day, you cannot read Bailey & Love cover to cover. You are not supposed to. Your goal is to build a strong, exam-ready mental model of Surgery — one that handles the theory paper, viva, and clinical case confidently.

---

## Resource Stack

| Resource | Role | When |
|----------|------|------|
| **Marrow** | Primary — fast, exam-focused | Daily Block 1 |
| **Bailey & Love** | Reference — depth and understanding | Daily Block 2 |
| **PYQs** | Testing — pattern recognition | Daily Block 4 |
| **This tracker** | System — accountability and review | Daily, ongoing |

---

## Why This Order?

**Marrow first** gives you the high-yield skeleton. You know what's important before you read Bailey.

**Bailey second** gives that skeleton flesh. Now when you read Bailey, you read with purpose — you already know what to look for.

**Revision third** reinforces before new information overwrites old.

**PYQs last** tests whether today's learning actually stuck, and trains your brain on the actual exam format.

---

## Managing Low-Energy Days

On days when motivation is low:
1. Skip Bailey & Love — do Marrow only (Block 1)
2. Do PYQs only (Block 4)
3. Do the revision block only (Block 3)

**Any one of these is better than zero.** Log it as ⚠️ in the streak tracker.

---

## The 3-Day Rule

If you miss a day, do not try to "catch up" the next day by doubling up. This leads to burnout.

Instead:
- Skip the missed topic OR
- Merge it briefly with the next related topic

The schedule is a guide, not a prison.

---

## Exam-Specific Tips

- **Short answer questions (SAQs)**: Use the framework — Definition → Classification → Pathophysiology → Clinical features → Investigations → Management → Complications → Prognosis
- **Viva**: Speak in structured paragraphs. Start with "Surgeon's definition", then classifications, then management.
- **MCQs**: Surgery MCQs repeat heavily. Marrow's question bank covers 90% of what appears.
